healthy tips

Fill half of your plate with colorful fruits and vegetables. They are high in vitamins, minerals, and fiber, and low in calories.

Choose whole grains over refined grains (e.g., brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat) to boost fiber intake and stabilize blood sugar.

Herbal teas and water-rich foods like cucumbers, watermelon, and oranges can also contribute to hydration.

Minimize your intake of processed foods, such as sugary snacks, sugary drinks, and ready-to-eat meals. These often contain excess salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats.

Limit added sugars by reducing the consumption of sugary drinks, snacks, and processed foods.

Fiber helps with digestion and can keep you feeling full longer. Foods like vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and legumes are great sources.
